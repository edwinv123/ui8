Chinchillas

Scientific Name: Chinchilla Lanigera

Classifications
Class: Mammals
Order: Rodents
Family: Chinchillidae
Genus: Chinchilla

Species: Short tailed chinchilla
Habitat: Mostly the chinchilla goes to Mountain shrub and grassland areas at elevations between 3000 and 4500 m. They make their dens moostly in rock crevices.
Chinchillas diet mostly is hay it is mostly their primary source of food.

Family Life: They are wildly social, living in family groups, which can form vast colonies, called herds, of over 100 individuals
Population: The exact population of wild chinchillas is unknown, but it is estimated that there are only 10,000 left in the world, they are also endangered.
Their life span is mostly around 8-10 years, their sizes are mostly around 23-38 cm long.

Fun Facts: They are the longest lived rodents, they have very dense fur, They are also nocturnal
